What We've Built

Two branded products on a single platform. College Ready Night serves grades 9–12 with adaptive math diagnostics tied to SAT, AP, and course-level tracks. Foundation Math Night serves grades 6–8 with the same adaptive engine, age-appropriate copy, and middle school tracks. Same codebase. Same database. Same admin portal. The platform routes each student to the right experience based on event type and grade.

This is not a quiz. It is a diagnostic engine that produces a math identity — something students want to share, parents want to understand, and we can use to drive engagement.

Section 02

The Student Flow

Eleven steps from first click to weekly practice. Fully automated — no tutor or staff required.

1
Landing Page + Registration ~2 min

Student arrives at the event-specific registration page. Picks their grade and track (SAT, AP Calculus, Grade 7, etc.). Enters name and email.

2
Adaptive Diagnostic Test 45–60 min

Module 1 (12 questions) → adaptive routing → Module 2 Easy or Hard (12 more). Each question tagged by FUAR dimension, difficulty (1–5), and topic. Timer runs per-module.

3
Mindset Quiz ~2 min

12 GRIC questions — Growth, Relevance, Interest, Confidence. 4-point scale. Self-report designed to take under 2 minutes.

4
Instant Archetype Reveal Instant

FUAR dominant dimension + GRIC mindset mode = one of 8 archetypes. Full-screen reveal with Greek symbol, name, tagline, superpower, kryptonite, "This Is You" scenarios, and mathematician pairings.

5
Share Card 1 tap

One-tap save/share card with archetype symbol, name, and tagline. Designed for social sharing — Instagram stories, text messages, school group chats.

6
Full Student Report Auto-generated

FUAR dimension scores (radar chart), topic-level heatmap showing strengths and gaps, overall score with school-level percentile context, and complete archetype profile.

7
Parent Report Auto-generated

Separate report for parents. SAT estimate or grade-level proficiency, FUAR breakdown in parent-friendly language, algebra readiness indicator (Grades 7–8), and specific next steps.

8
Persistent Dashboard Ongoing

Practice plan, progress tracking, FUAR radar chart, and score trajectory over time. HS: SAT score progress. MS: grade-level proficiency progress.

9
Daily Practice Ongoing

Adaptive practice questions targeting the student's weakest FUAR dimensions and topics. Pulled from the full question bank — not just the 24 test questions.

10
Practice Review Per question

Every practice question has a step-by-step worked solution with wrong-answer analysis — not just "the answer is B" but the full approach and why each wrong answer is wrong.

11
Weekly Assessments + Archetype Evolution Weekly

Weekly mini-assessments recalculate FUAR scores and can trigger archetype evolution. A student who was Gamma (brilliant but inconsistent) might become Alpha (brilliant AND disciplined) as Fluency improves.

Section 05

The FUAR Framework

Cuemath's proprietary framework for decomposing mathematical ability into four measurable dimensions. Every question is tagged to exactly one dimension — enabling per-dimension scoring, not just an aggregate number.

F
Fluency
Speed and accuracy on practiced procedures. Can the student execute reliably under time pressure?
"Can you execute this reliably?"
U
Understanding
Conceptual depth, transfer to new contexts. Does the student know WHY, not just HOW?
"Do you know why this works?"
A
Application
Translating real-world problems into math. Can they model messy situations mathematically?
"Can you use this in the real world?"
R
Reasoning
Logical deduction, proof, novel problem-solving. Can they solve things nobody has taught them?
"Can you figure out what you were never taught?"

GRIC Mindset Layer

After the diagnostic, students answer 12 questions measuring Growth, Relevance, Interest, and Confidence. Dominant dimensions determine mindset mode.

GRIC Dimensions
G Growth — "I can get better at math through effort" vs. fixed mindset
R Relevance — "Math matters for my future" vs. disconnection
I Interest — Intrinsic curiosity about math
C Confidence — Self-belief when facing hard problems
Mindset Modes
Engaged
Growth or Interest dominant — intrinsically motivated
Guarded
Relevance or Confidence dominant — extrinsic or protective motivation
