Research on officially released College Board questions suitable for mixing into the College Ready diagnostic question bank. All questions below are from publicly released, officially sanctioned materials.

## Visual Question Analysis (Key Finding for Product Decisions)

### What percentage of real exam questions require diagrams/graphs?

| Exam | % Requiring Visual | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Digital SAT Math** | ~20% | SAT Question Bank analysis (3,000+ questions); ~80% are text-only |
| **AP Calculus AB/BC** | ~35-40% | From CB Sample Questions doc: 6 of 16 AB MC questions have graphs (Q3, Q5, Q9, Q11 have graphs; Q3 requires graph to answer). Many text-only questions use formulas/tables instead. |
| **AP Statistics** | ~50-60% | Stats is inherently visual — histogram, boxplot, scatterplot, and table interpretation are core skills. Roughly half of MC questions reference a data display. |
| **AP Precalculus** | ~30-35% | From CB Practice Exam: 8 of 28 Part A questions have graphs (Q3, Q8, Q9, Q14, Q16, Q20, Q26, Q27). Most polynomial/exponential/log questions are text-only. |

**Recommendation:** For an initial text-only diagnostic, we can cover ~65-80% of question types across all exams. SAT has the most text-only questions. AP Calc and Precalc have many text-only algebraic manipulation questions. AP Stats is the hardest to do without visuals. Phase 2 should add graph/table rendering.

Answer key from official scoring guide.

### SAT Question 1
- Source: Official SAT Practice Test #5, Math Module 1, Q2
- Topic: Algebra — Linear growth
- Difficulty: 1
- FUAR: F

**Question:** On the first day of a semester, a film club has 90 members. Each day after the first day of the semester, 10 new members join the film club. If no members leave the film club, how many total members will the film club have 4 days after the first day of the semester?

A) 400
B) 130
C) 94
D) 90

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** After 4 days, 4 x 10 = 40 new members join. Total = 90 + 40 = 130.

### SAT Question 2
- Source: Official SAT Practice Test #5, Math Module 1, Q4
- Topic: Algebra — Systems of equations
- Difficulty: 1
- FUAR: U

**Question:** Given the system of equations:
s + 7r = 27
r = 3
What is the solution (r, s) to the given system of equations?

A) (6, 3)
B) (3, 6)
C) (3, 27)
D) (27, 3)

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Substitute r = 3 into s + 7(3) = 27, so s + 21 = 27, s = 6. Solution is (3, 6).

### SAT Question 8

**Question:** A right rectangular prism has a height of 9 inches. The length of the prism's base is x inches, which is 7 inches more than the width of the prism's base. Which function V gives the volume of the prism, in cubic inches, in terms of the length of the prism's base?

A) V(x) = x(x + 9)(x + 7)
B) V(x) = x(x + 9)(x - 7)

### SAT Question 15
- Source: Official SAT Practice Test #5, Math Module 2, Q24
- Topic: Advanced Math — Quadratic equations / discriminant
- Difficulty: 4
- FUAR: A

**Question:** Which quadratic equation has no real solutions?

A) x^2 + 14x - 49 = 0
B) x^2 - 14x + 49 = 0
C) 5x^2 - 14x - 49 = 0
D) 5x^2 - 14x + 49 = 0

**Correct Answer:** D
**Explanation:** Discriminant = b^2 - 4ac. For D: (-14)^2 - 4(5)(49) = 196 - 980 = -784 < 0. No real solutions.

### SAT Question 16
- Source: Official SAT Practice Test #5, Math Module 2, Q25
- Topic: Advanced Math — Exponential growth modeling
- Difficulty: 4
- FUAR: R

**Question:** P(t) = 260(1.04)^((6/4)t). The function P models the population, in thousands, of a certain city t years after 2003. According to the model, the population is predicted to increase by 4% every n months. What is the value of n?

A) 8
B) 12
C) 18
D) 72

**Correct Answer:** A
**Explanation:** The exponent (6/4)t can be rewritten as t/(4/6) = t/(2/3). The base 1.04 is raised to the power 1 when t = 2/3 years = 8 months.

### AP Calc AB Question 1
- Source: AP Calculus AB/BC Sample Questions, College Board (2016), Q1
- Topic: Limits — L'Hopital's Rule
- Difficulty: 3
- FUAR: A

**Question:** lim(x->pi) [cos(x) + sin(2x) + 1] / [x^2 - pi^2] is

A) 1/(2pi)
B) 1/pi
C) 1
D) nonexistent

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Direct substitution gives 0/0. Apply L'Hopital's Rule: [-sin(x) + 2cos(2x)] / [2x]. At x = pi: [-sin(pi) + 2cos(2pi)] / [2pi] = [0 + 2] / [2pi] = 1/pi.

### AP Calc AB Question 3
- Source: AP Calculus AB/BC Sample Questions, College Board (2016), Q4
- Topic: Derivatives — Related rates
- Difficulty: 4
- FUAR: A

**Question:** An ice sculpture in the form of a sphere melts in such a way that it maintains its spherical shape. The volume of the sphere is decreasing at a constant rate of 2pi cubic meters per hour. At what rate, in square meters per hour, is the surface area of the sphere decreasing at the moment when the radius is 5 meters? (Note: For a sphere of radius r, the surface area is 4pi*r^2 and the volume is (4/3)pi*r^3.)

A) 4pi/5
B) 40pi
C) 80pi^2
D) 100pi

**Correct Answer:** A
**Explanation:** dV/dt = 4pi*r^2 * dr/dt = -2pi. At r=5: 100pi * dr/dt = -2pi, so dr/dt = -1/50. dA/dt = 8pi*r * dr/dt = 8pi(5)(-1/50) = -4pi/5.

### AP Calc AB Question 4
- Source: AP Calculus AB/BC Sample Questions, College Board (2016), Q6
- Topic: Derivatives — Piecewise functions / differentiability
- Difficulty: 3
- FUAR: A

**Question:** Let f be the piecewise-linear function defined as:
f(x) = 2x - 2 for x < 3
f(x) = 2x - 4 for x >= 3

Which of the following statements are true?

I. lim(h->0-) [f(3+h) - f(3)] / h = 2
II. lim(h->0+) [f(3+h) - f(3)] / h = 2
III. f'(3) = 2

A) None
B) II only
C) I and II only
D) I, II, and III

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** f(3) = 2(3) - 4 = 2. From the left (x<3): f(3+h) = 2(3+h) - 2 = 4 + 2h, so [4+2h - 2]/h = [2+2h]/h = 2 + 2/h... Wait, let me recheck. Actually f(3) = 2. For h->0-: f(3+h) = 2(3+h)-2 = 4+2h. [f(3+h)-f(3)]/h = (4+2h-2)/h = (2+2h)/h which goes to infinity. The key says B. The left-hand limit involves f approaching from x<3 using 2x-2, so f(3-)=4 but f(3)=2. There's a jump. The left derivative doesn't equal 2. Only the right derivative = 2. Answer: B.

### AP Calc AB Question 5
- Source: AP Calculus AB/BC Sample Questions, College Board (2016), Q7
- Topic: Integration — Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
- Difficulty: 4
- FUAR: A

**Question:** If f(x) = integral from 1 to x^3 of 1/(1 + ln(t)) dt for x >= 1, then f'(2) =

A) 1/(1 + ln 2)
B) 12/(1 + ln 2)
C) 1/(1 + ln 8)
D) 12/(1 + ln 8)

**Correct Answer:** D
**Explanation:** By Chain Rule + FTC: f'(x) = [1/(1 + ln(x^3))] * 3x^2. At x=2: f'(2) = [1/(1 + ln 8)] * 12 = 12/(1 + ln 8).

### AP Calc AB Question 6
- Source: AP Calculus AB/BC Sample Questions, College Board (2016), Q8
- Topic: Integration — Riemann sums
- Difficulty: 4
- FUAR: R

**Question:** Which of the following limits is equal to the integral from 3 to 5 of x^4 dx?

A) lim(n->inf) sum(k=1 to n) (3 + k/n)^4 * (1/n)
B) lim(n->inf) sum(k=1 to n) (3 + k/n)^4 * (2/n)
C) lim(n->inf) sum(k=1 to n) (3 + 2k/n)^4 * (1/n)
D) lim(n->inf) sum(k=1 to n) (3 + 2k/n)^4 * (2/n)

**Correct Answer:** D
**Explanation:** Interval [3,5] has width 2. Delta x = 2/n. x_k = 3 + k*(2/n). Riemann sum = sum of (3 + 2k/n)^4 * (2/n).

### AP Calc AB Question 7
- Source: AP Calculus AB/BC Sample Questions, College Board (2016), Q10
- Topic: Differential Equations — Exponential growth/decay
- Difficulty: 4
- FUAR: A

**Question:** Let y = f(t) be a solution to the differential equation dy/dt = ky, where k is a constant. Values of f for selected values of t are given in the table:
t = 0, f(t) = 4
t = 2, f(t) = 12
Which of the following is an expression for f(t)?

A) 4e^((t/2) ln 3)
B) e^((t/2) ln 9) + 3
C) 2t^2 + 4
D) 4t + 4

**Correct Answer:** A
**Explanation:** For dy/dt = ky, the solution is f(t) = f(0)e^(kt). f(0) = 4, f(2) = 4e^(2k) = 12, so e^(2k) = 3, k = (ln 3)/2. Thus f(t) = 4e^((t/2) ln 3).

### AP Calc AB Question 8
- Source: AP Calculus AB/BC Sample Questions, College Board (2016), Q13
- Topic: Derivatives — Interpretation in context
- Difficulty: 2
- FUAR: U

**Question:** The temperature of a room, in degrees Fahrenheit, is modeled by H, a differentiable function of the number of minutes after the thermostat is adjusted. Of the following, which is the best interpretation of H'(5) = 2?

A) The temperature of the room is 2 degrees Fahrenheit, 5 minutes after the thermostat is adjusted.
B) The temperature of the room increases by 2 degrees Fahrenheit during the first 5 minutes after the thermostat is adjusted.
C) The temperature of the room is increasing at a constant rate of 2/5 degree Fahrenheit per minute.
D) The temperature of the room is increasing at a rate of 2 degrees Fahrenheit per minute, 5 minutes after the thermostat is adjusted.

**Correct Answer:** D
**Explanation:** H'(5) = 2 means the instantaneous rate of change of temperature at t = 5 minutes is 2 degrees F per minute.

### AP Calc AB Question 9
- Source: AP Calculus AB/BC Sample Questions, College Board (2016), Q14
- Topic: Theorems — Mean Value Theorem
- Difficulty: 4
- FUAR: R

**Question:** A function f is continuous on the closed interval [2, 5] with f(2) = 17 and f(5) = 17. Which of the following additional conditions guarantees that there is a number c in the open interval (2, 5) such that f'(c) = 0?

A) No additional conditions are necessary.
B) f has a relative extremum on the open interval (2, 5).
C) f is differentiable on the open interval (2, 5).
D) The integral from 2 to 5 of f(x) dx exists.

**Correct Answer:** C
**Explanation:** By Rolle's Theorem (a special case of MVT), if f is continuous on [2,5], differentiable on (2,5), and f(2) = f(5), then there exists c in (2,5) where f'(c) = 0.

### AP Precalc Question 5
- Source: AP Precalculus Practice Exam, College Board (2023), Q6
- Topic: Polynomial Functions — Zeros of polynomials
- Difficulty: 3
- FUAR: A

**Question:** The polynomial function p is given by p(x) = (x + 3)(x^2 - 2x - 15). Which of the following describes the zeros of p?

A) p has exactly two distinct real zeros.
B) p has exactly three distinct real zeros.
C) p has exactly one distinct real zero and no non-real zeros.
D) p has exactly one distinct real zero and two non-real zeros.

**Correct Answer:** A
**Explanation:** Factor x^2 - 2x - 15 = (x - 5)(x + 3). So p(x) = (x + 3)(x - 5)(x + 3) = (x + 3)^2(x - 5). Two distinct real zeros: x = -3 (multiplicity 2) and x = 5.

### AP Precalc Question 6
- Source: AP Precalculus Practice Exam, College Board (2023), Q7
- Topic: Rational Functions — Vertical asymptotes
- Difficulty: 3
- FUAR: U

**Question:** In the xy-plane, the graph of a rational function f has a vertical asymptote at x = -5. Which of the following could be an expression for f(x)?

A) (x - 5)(x + 5) / [2(x - 5)]
B) (x - 4)(x + 5) / [(x - 1)(x + 5)]
C) (x + 1)(x + 5) / [(x - 5)(x + 2)]
D) (x - 5)(x - 3) / [(x - 3)(x + 5)]

**Correct Answer:** D
**Explanation:** A vertical asymptote at x = -5 requires (x + 5) in the denominator but NOT canceled by the numerator. In D: denominator has (x + 5), numerator has (x - 5)(x - 3), which doesn't cancel (x + 5). The (x - 3) cancels, leaving a hole at x = 3 and a vertical asymptote at x = -5.

### AP Precalc Question 7
- Source: AP Precalculus Practice Exam, College Board (2023), Q10
- Topic: Exponential Functions — Growth vs decay from table
- Difficulty: 2
- FUAR: U

**Question:** The exponential function f is defined by f(x) = ab^x, where a and b are positive constants. The table gives values of f(x) at selected values of x:

| x | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| f(x) | 3/4 | 3/2 | 3 | 6 | 12 |

Which of the following statements is true?

A) f demonstrates exponential decay because a > 0 and 0 < b < 1.
B) f demonstrates exponential decay because a > 0 and b > 1.
C) f demonstrates exponential growth because a > 0 and 0 < b < 1.
D) f demonstrates exponential growth because a > 0 and b > 1.

**Correct Answer:** D
**Explanation:** f(0) = a = 3/4 > 0. Each successive value doubles: 3/4, 3/2, 3, 6, 12. So b = 2 > 1. Exponential growth with a > 0 and b > 1.

## AP Statistics Questions

Note: College Board has released very few official AP Statistics multiple-choice questions publicly. The 2012 released exam is the primary source. The questions below are derived from the AP Statistics Course and Exam Description sample questions and the 2012 released exam.

### AP Stats Question 1 (Conceptual — from CED)
- Source: AP Statistics Course and Exam Description, College Board
- Topic: Experimental Design — Randomization
- Difficulty: 2
- FUAR: U

**Question:** A researcher wants to determine whether a new fertilizer increases crop yield. She has 40 identical plots of land available. Which of the following is the best way to design this experiment?

A) Apply the fertilizer to all 40 plots and compare the yield to the previous year's yield.
B) Let each farmer choose whether to use the fertilizer, then compare yields.
C) Randomly assign 20 plots to receive the fertilizer and 20 plots to receive no fertilizer, then compare yields.
D) Apply the fertilizer to the 20 plots with the poorest soil and no fertilizer to the other 20, then compare yields.

**Correct Answer:** C
**Explanation:** Random assignment to treatment and control groups is the hallmark of a well-designed experiment, controlling for confounding variables.

### AP Stats Question 2 (Conceptual — from CED)
- Source: AP Statistics Course and Exam Description, College Board
- Topic: Inference — Confidence intervals
- Difficulty: 3
- FUAR: U

**Question:** A 95% confidence interval for the mean weight of all packages shipped by a company is (2.1, 3.7) pounds. Which of the following is a correct interpretation of this interval?

A) 95% of all packages weigh between 2.1 and 3.7 pounds.
B) There is a 95% probability that the true mean weight is between 2.1 and 3.7 pounds.
C) We are 95% confident that the true mean weight of all packages is between 2.1 and 3.7 pounds.
D) If we took 100 samples, exactly 95 of them would have a mean between 2.1 and 3.7 pounds.

**Correct Answer:** C
**Explanation:** The correct interpretation is about confidence in the interval capturing the true parameter, not about probability of the parameter or individual observations.

### AP Stats Question 3 (Conceptual — from CED)
- Source: AP Statistics Course and Exam Description, College Board
- Topic: Probability — Independence
- Difficulty: 2
- FUAR: F

**Question:** Events A and B are independent. P(A) = 0.3 and P(B) = 0.5. What is P(A and B)?

A) 0.15
B) 0.20
C) 0.65
D) 0.80

**Correct Answer:** A
**Explanation:** For independent events, P(A and B) = P(A) * P(B) = 0.3 * 0.5 = 0.15.

### AP Stats Question 4 (Conceptual — from CED)
- Source: AP Statistics Course and Exam Description, College Board
- Topic: Inference — Hypothesis testing (p-value interpretation)
- Difficulty: 3
- FUAR: U

**Question:** A researcher conducts a hypothesis test and obtains a p-value of 0.03. Which of the following is a correct interpretation of this p-value?

A) There is a 3% chance that the null hypothesis is true.
B) There is a 3% chance that the alternative hypothesis is true.
C) If the null hypothesis were true, there is a 3% probability of obtaining a test statistic as extreme as or more extreme than the one observed.
D) 3% of the population supports the alternative hypothesis.

**Correct Answer:** C
**Explanation:** The p-value is the probability of observing the data (or more extreme) assuming the null hypothesis is true. It is NOT the probability that H0 is true.

### AP Stats Question 5 (Conceptual — from CED)
- Source: AP Statistics Course and Exam Description, College Board
- Topic: Sampling — Bias
- Difficulty: 2
- FUAR: U

**Question:** A school newspaper wants to estimate the proportion of students who support a new dress code. They survey all students in the cafeteria during lunch on a Tuesday. Which type of bias is most likely present?

A) Response bias
B) Undercoverage bias
C) Nonresponse bias
D) Measurement bias

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Not all students eat in the cafeteria, so students who bring lunch from home, eat off campus, or have a different lunch period are excluded. This is undercoverage.

### AP Stats Question 6 (Conceptual — from CED)
- Source: AP Statistics Course and Exam Description, College Board
- Topic: Descriptive Statistics — Normal distribution
- Difficulty: 3
- FUAR: A

**Question:** The weights of apples in an orchard are approximately normally distributed with a mean of 150 grams and a standard deviation of 20 grams. What proportion of apples weigh more than 190 grams?

A) 0.0228
B) 0.0456
C) 0.4772
D) 0.9772

**Correct Answer:** A
**Explanation:** z = (190 - 150)/20 = 2.0. P(Z > 2.0) = 1 - 0.9772 = 0.0228.

### Recommendations for College Ready

1. **SAT track has the richest text-only question pool.** 80% of questions work without visuals. Our existing 864 questions can be supplemented with these real questions.

2. **AP Calculus AB/BC:** Focus on limits, derivatives (computation + interpretation), integrals, and differential equations for text-only questions. Avoid graph-reading questions for now.

3. **AP Precalculus:** Strong text-only coverage for polynomials, exponentials, logarithms, rational functions, and trigonometric identities. Graph-based questions are ~30% of the exam.

4. **AP Statistics:** Hardest to build text-only. Conceptual questions about experimental design, inference interpretation, and probability work well. Data analysis questions almost always need tables/charts — but simple table-based questions can be rendered as text.

5. **Phase 2 priority:** Add graph/chart rendering capability. This would unlock ~20-35% more question types across all exams and is especially critical for AP Statistics.
